How to Read Your BaZi Chart:
A Step-by-Step Guide for Beginners

Published June 5, 2026 · 9 min read · By Destiny Code

Your BaZi chart — also called the Four Pillars of Destiny — is a cosmic map of your birth moment. At first glance, it looks like a grid of unfamiliar Chinese characters and symbols. But once you understand the structure, reading your BaZi chart becomes an illuminating practice of self-discovery. This guide walks you through every part of the chart, step by step.

If you are completely new to BaZi, we recommend reading What Is BaZi? The Four Pillars of Destiny Explained first for essential context.

The Anatomy of a BaZi Chart

A BaZi chart is arranged in four columns (pillars), each containing two rows — a Heavenly Stem (top) and an Earthly Branch (bottom). The four pillars represent different time units of your birth:

Pillar Represents What It Governs
Year Pillar Birth year Ancestry, childhood, external environment
Month Pillar Birth month Parents, career, early adulthood
Day Pillar Birth day Self, spouse, core identity
Hour Pillar Birth hour Children, legacy, inner world

Step 1: Find Your Day Master

1

Locate the Day Master

The Day Master is the Heavenly Stem of your Day Pillar — the top character of the third column. This is you. It represents your core self, personality, and natural tendencies. There are 10 possible Day Masters: Yang Wood, Yin Wood, Yang Fire, Yin Fire, Yang Earth, Yin Earth, Yang Metal, Yin Metal, Yang Water, and Yin Water.

Step 2: Identify All Five Elements in Your Chart

Each Heavenly Stem and Earthly Branch carries one or more of the Five Elements. Count how many times each element — Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water — appears across all eight positions (4 stems + 4 branches). This gives you your element distribution.

Step 3: Assess the Strength of Your Day Master

2

Determine Day Master Strength

This is the most critical reading skill. A strong Day Master has ample support from the same element or its generating element (e.g., a Wood Day Master surrounded by Water and Wood). A weak Day Master lacks support and may be overwhelmed by controlling elements. Your ideal balance dictates which elements are favorable (useful) and which are unfavorable (harmful) for you.

Rule of thumb: A strong Day Master needs to be controlled or weakened (Metal or Fire for Wood types). A weak Day Master needs to be supported or strengthened (Water or Wood for Wood types).

Step 4: Examine the Heavenly Stems

The four Heavenly Stems sit on the top row of each pillar. They represent the visible, conscious forces in your life — what people see, how you act, and your outward expression. Look for special Stem combinations known as Heavenly Stem Combinations (天干合), which indicate transformative energies or significant relationships.

Step 5: Decode the Earthly Branches

The four Earthly Branches on the bottom row are your hidden, subconscious terrain. Each Branch contains Hidden Stems — usually 1 to 3 sub-elements that reveal deeper layers of your personality. For example, the Rat (子) contains only Yang Water, while the Dragon (辰) contains Yang Earth, Yin Wood, and Yin Water.

Branches also form special relationships:

  • Six Combinations (六合) — two branches merging to form a new element
  • Three Harmonies (三合局) — three branches forming a powerful alliance
  • Clashes (冲) — opposite branches creating tension and change
  • Punishments (害) — subtle friction affecting health or relationships

Step 6: Understand the Luck Cycles

3

Read the Luck Pillars

Beyond the fixed Four Pillars, your BaZi chart includes 10-Year Luck Cycles — rolling pillars that change approximately every decade. Your current Luck Pillar colors the opportunities and challenges of your present life phase. A favorable Luck Pillar can turn a difficult fixed chart into a golden decade; an unfavorable one can suppress even a strong natal chart.

Step 7: Apply the Reading to Life Areas

Once you grasp the chart's structural meaning, you can apply it to specific domains:

  • Career: Which elements dominate? A strong Metal chart suits law or finance; a strong Wood chart suits education or entrepreneurship. See BaZi Career Path.
  • Love & Relationships: Examine the Day Branch (spouse palace) and the Year Branch (external relationships). Read BaZi Love Compatibility.
  • Wealth: Look at the elements that "produce" wealth for your Day Master. Consult BaZi Wealth Luck.
  • Health: Element imbalances point to organ systems and potential vulnerabilities. See BaZi Health Elements.

Common Beginner Mistakes

  1. Ignoring the Hour Pillar. If you don't know your birth hour, you are missing 25% of your chart. Many online calculators guess at noon, which may be wrong.
  2. Over-focusing on zodiac animals. The Earthly Branch animals (Rat, Ox, Tiger...) are secondary to the elemental interactions.
  3. Skipping Day Master strength. Without assessing strength, you cannot determine which elements are genuinely favorable.
  4. Reading in isolation. A single clash or combination means little without the full context of the chart's season and Day Master.
